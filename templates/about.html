I am a research scientist at DeepMind in London, working on using neuroscience-inspired approaches to understand and build better thinking systems.

I recently completed my PhD in the lab of Chris Harvey at Harvard Medical School. For my thesis work, I worked on methods to understand how neuronal circuits perform the computations necessary for complex behavior. In particular, my research focused on how parietal cortex contributes to evidence accumulation decision-making. I am also interested in using novel analysis techniques from machine learning to us to better understand neuronal circuits and vice-versa. I am especially excited by thinking how insights from real neuronal circuits can help us to improve artificial neural networks and to towards AGI.

For my undergraduate work, I attended UCSD where I worked with Fred Gage to investigate the role of REST/NRSF in adult neurogenesis.
